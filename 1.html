Stroke Status vs Residence Type and Work Type

This sankey diagram displays stroke status across work types and residence types.

Key observations:

  • - Urban residents: 94.1% have not had a stroke, while approximately 5.9% have experienced a stroke
  • - Rural residents: 94.9% of rural residents have not had strokes, while approximately 5.1% have experienced a stroke
  • - Self-employed individuals show the highest stroke prevalence at 7.9% (meaning 92.1% have not had a stroke)
  • - Private sector workers have a stroke rate of 5.1% (94.9% without stroke)
  • - Government employees demonstrate a slightly lower stroke rate at 5% (95% without stroke)
  • - Never worked category shows 0% stroke cases (100% without stroke)


While this shows variations in stroke prevalence across work types, these differences are relatively low (only a maximum of about 3% difference among adults). The data reveals minimal difference in stroke rates between urban and rural settings, suggesting geographical location has little impact on stroke occurrence.
